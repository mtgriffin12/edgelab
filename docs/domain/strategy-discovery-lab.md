# Strategy Discovery Lab

## Purpose

The Strategy Discovery Lab helps EdgeLab discover, classify, and track strategy ideas before they
become formal strategy specs. It separates idea generation from evidence.

## Known Strategies And New Hypotheses

Known strategy families are allowed. They may be useful when current conditions resemble the
environments where they historically had a chance. They still must prove current fit and beat
simple baselines.

Novel hypotheses are allowed too, but novelty is not the objective. Durable edge is the objective.
New ideas usually carry higher curve-fit risk and need stricter evidence.

## Baseline Discipline

Adaptive strategies must beat simpler baselines. A sentiment-aware breakout, for example, must beat
a plain breakout. A broad-fear pullback idea must beat an ordinary relative-strength pullback.

If a complex idea cannot beat the simpler version, complexity is a liability.

## Current-Regime Fit

Current-regime fit asks a plain-English question: does the current environment look like the kind
of environment where this idea historically had a chance?

Phase 5C only creates static scaffolding for this question. It does not use live market data.

## Experiment Ledger

Failed ideas must remain visible. The experiment ledger should remember failed, inconclusive, and
not-run experiments so EdgeLab does not rediscover the same weak ideas without context.

## Plain-English Requirement

Every discovery record should explain:

- What market behavior it tries to exploit.
- Why that behavior might exist.
- Why it might work now.
- What simpler baseline it must beat.
- What evidence would disprove it.
- When it should not be used.

No discovery record should imply that an idea is proven, ready for real money, or actionable.
